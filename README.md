# Federal Government Budget 2026- Analysis & Insights

Our team was handed one of the most consequential datasets in Nigeria right now: the 2026 Federal Government Budget. No template. No guided questions. Just raw data, a brief, and a mandate to find the story inside the numbers; and tell it in a way every Nigerian can understand.

We called ourselves The Data Refinery, and we were contracted (in simulation) by Channels Television to produce a prime-time data report. The title we landed on: "Paying for Yesterday with Tomorrow's Money; Where The Average Nigerian Money Goes."

Here is what the data actually says:

📊 THE BIG PICTURE
Total budget: ₦58.47 trillion. Expected revenue: ₦33.20 trillion. That gap of ₦25.27 trillion  has to be borrowed. In plain language: for every ₦100 Nigeria plans to spend in 2026, it only has ₦57. The other ₦43 is debt. And that deficit nearly doubled in a single budget cycle; up 93% from 2025.

💸 DEBT IS EATING THE BUDGET ALIVE
The second largest item in this budget is not education, roads, or hospitals. It is debt service; ₦15.91 trillion, or 27.2% of the entire budget; before a single kobo reaches any ministry. We are spending more on past borrowing than on running the country today.

🛢️ THE OIL PROBLEM
Over 60% of Nigeria's expected revenue flows from the Federation Account, which is primarily funded by oil and gas. One commodity. One vulnerability. When global prices fall or production drops, the entire budget plan unravels.

🏗️ THE GENUINE GOOD NEWS
Capital expenditure hit ₦23.21 trillion; a 69% increase from 2025 and the highest development spending in recent memory. Works, Power, and Agriculture saw major capital jumps. If this money is executed well, it could mean real infrastructure change. The word is IF; Q3 2025 showed only 17.7% capital budget release.

📚 THE NUMBER THAT SHOULD BOTHER EVERYONE
Education: 3.3% of the budget. Health: 3.3%. UNESCO recommends 17.5% for education. The WHO recommends 15% for health. Meanwhile, the security cluster absorbs over ₦5 trillion on the recurrent side alone; more than education and health combined. That is a political choice, not a resource problem.

🔍 WHAT WE DID
We sourced directly from the Budget Office of the Federation, cross-verified with BudgIT and PwC Nigeria, cleaned and structured the raw Appropriation Bill into 6 analysis-ready CSV files, built a 3-page interactive Power BI dashboard, and delivered a full investigative story report; all in plain language for a general audience.
